# Executive Summary

**Submitted to:** Meridian Components, Inc.
**RFP #:** MC-2026-0417
**Submitted by:** [Your Firm Name]
**Date:** April 28, 2026

---

Meridian Components operates a fast-moving, multi-warehouse business where accurate inventory data isn't a nice-to-have — it's what your operations team uses to make decisions every day. We understand that, and we've built our proposed approach around delivering reliable, usable software as quickly as possible.

Our approach has four parts, in the order that delivers the most value fastest:

1. **Establish a test baseline (R3).** We will define and deliver automated browser test coverage for all critical operational flows before we touch anything else. This gives your IT team the confidence to approve changes safely — and it gives us a foundation to build on throughout the engagement.

2. **Remediate the Reports module (R1).** We will conduct a thorough audit and resolve every defect we find, working against documented acceptance criteria. Your team will know exactly what was fixed and how to verify it.

3. **Build the Restocking view (R2).** We will deliver a new view that generates actionable purchase order recommendations based on current stock levels, demand signals, and an operator-supplied budget ceiling. We will work directly with your operations team to make sure the output matches how they actually make reorder decisions.

4. **Deliver architecture documentation (R4)** produced from a live review of the codebase — accurate, current, and ready for your IT team to own.

The four required items above are scoped for delivery within a six-week engagement. Weeks five and six are reserved for the desired items — UI modernization (D1), extended i18n for the Tokyo team (D2), and dark mode for low-light floor stations (D3) — provided the required scope is accepted and stable by end of week four.

We are a small, senior team. You will work with the people who wrote this proposal — not a delivery team assembled after contract signature. We scope conservatively and deliver what we commit to.

---

*Assumptions:* Meridian does not have a documented defect log for the Reports module; our audit will establish the full list. Demand forecast data is available via the existing API. "Critical flows" for R3 are defined in our Technical Approach (§2) and subject to IT sign-off before testing begins.
